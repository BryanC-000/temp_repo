In Situ Carcinoma is a condition in which abnormal cells are found in the tissues of the breast. These abnormal cells may become cancer and spread to nearby normal tissue. In general, carcinoma in situ is the earliest form of cancer, and is considered stage 0 cancer that has not yet spread. There are 2 types of breast carcinoma in situ: ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS) and Paget disease of the nipple. DCIS is a condition in which the abnormal cells are found in the lining of a breast duct. The abnormal cells have not spread outside the duct to other tissues in the breast. Paget disease of the nipple is a condition in which abnormal cells are found in the skin cells of the nipple and may spread to the areola. Patients with Paget disease of the nipple may also have DCIS or invasive breast cancer in the same breast. Also called stage 0 breast carcinoma in situ. To confirm your results and get treatment, please book an appointment with your doctor as soon as possible.
